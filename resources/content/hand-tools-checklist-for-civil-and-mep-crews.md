Hand tools remain critical for quality finishing and quick correction work.

## Daily toolkit baseline
Ensure each crew has core measuring, fastening, cutting, and alignment tools.

## Inspect wear points weekly
Damaged grips, worn cutting edges, and bent handles impact safety and finish quality.

## Keep duplicate high-usage tools
A small duplicate set avoids workflow bottlenecks during peak activity windows.

## Train for tool care
Simple maintenance habits significantly improve tool life and reduce replacement spend.
