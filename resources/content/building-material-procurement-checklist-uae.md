A disciplined buying process helps contractors avoid stockouts, missed deadlines, and budget leakage.

## 1) Validate scope against the latest drawings
Always verify your material list against the most recent IFC drawings and approved submittals before ordering.

## 2) Split items by urgency and lead time
Group materials into immediate, short-cycle, and long-cycle categories. This avoids site stoppages caused by one delayed line item.

## 3) Confirm compatibility
Check fixing systems, tool accessories, and consumables for compatibility. Small mismatches create major on-site delays.

## 4) Build a replenishment rhythm
For recurring items, use weekly or bi-weekly replenishment instead of large, infrequent purchases.

## 5) Track usage variance
Compare planned vs actual usage. Variance trends are the fastest way to improve procurement precision over time.
